Early Life

Miles Dewey Davis III was born on May 26, 1926, in Alton, Illinois, USA. He was raised in a middle-class African-American family and grew up in East St. Louis, Illinois.

Career

Miles Davis was a pioneering jazz musician, trumpeter, and composer. He played a major role in the development of several jazz styles, including bebop, cool jazz, hard bop, modal jazz, and jazz fusion.

Legacy

Miles Davis is considered one of the most influential musicians of the 20th century. His innovative approach to jazz and fearless exploration of new musical territories continue to inspire musicians across genres.

Personal Life

Miles Davis was known for his complex personality and turbulent personal life. He struggled with drug addiction and had a tumultuous romantic life. Despite his personal challenges, he remained dedicated to his music.
